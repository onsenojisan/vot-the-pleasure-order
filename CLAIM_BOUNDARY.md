# Claim Boundary

This document defines the current public claim boundary for VOT / The Pleasure Order.

For the global current status, see [Current Status](CURRENT_STATUS.md). This document is narrower: it preserves claim discipline for archived evidence, especially the L4 structural-proxy record, and for any stronger future empirical claim.

## Current L4 Archived Claim

The L4 archived Zenodo record supports the following bounded claim:

```text
L4 rule-conditional D2+ structural-proxy support
```

Record:

```text
https://zenodo.org/records/20728608
https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.20728608
```

This is a measurement-design and structural-proxy claim. It means that, in the released package, fixed public time-series rules support a distinction between repair-like recovery and sustained non-repair reorganization across the tested cases and controls.

It does not summarize the whole public evidence base. For the full record index, see [Zenodo Records](ZENODO_RECORDS.md).

## What This Project Does Not Currently Claim

The project does not currently claim:

- proof of VOT / The Pleasure Order;
- strong D2+ confirmation beyond the archived L4 rule-conditional structural-proxy level;
- direct pleasure measurement;
- direct suffering measurement;
- direct acceptance/refusal measurement;
- direct Omega measurement;
- direct O/M/A validation;
- causal identification;
- prediction of collapse, events, or system failure;
- clinical validation or broad generality.

Older Omega materials should be read as historical, methodological, or exploratory unless explicitly reauthorized.

## Current Allowed Framing

Current public framing may describe:

- working definitions;
- claim boundaries;
- empirical-readiness requirements;
- historical status of earlier Omega materials;
- the archived L4 measurement-design package on Zenodo;
- future conditions required before stronger empirical claims.

The L4 record may be described as structural-proxy support. It should not be converted into direct theory proof, direct pleasure/suffering measurement, direct Omega/O-M-A validation, or causality.

## Requirements For Stronger Future Empirical Claims

Any stronger future empirical claim would require, before evaluation:

- fixed definitions;
- independent outcome labels or preregistered public-data labels;
- non-circularity checks;
- sufficient sample size or a justified public-data design;
- a validation design;
- direct or defensible proxy treatment of pleasure, suffering, acceptance/refusal, and Omega/O/M/A;
- clear treatment of null, weak, or conflicting outcomes.

No stronger empirical claim should be made from historical Omega materials alone.
