# Claim Boundary

This document defines the current public claim boundary for VOT / The Pleasure Order during the restart phase.

## What This Project Does Not Currently Claim

The project does not currently claim:

- empirical validation of VOT / The Pleasure Order;
- empirical validation of Omega;
- proof of VOT, The Pleasure Order, pleasure, directionality, or Omega;
- prediction of collapse, events, or system failure;
- causal explanation established by empirical results;
- an empirically established measurement model for Omega;
- that older Omega empirical or reproducibility materials are the current project center.

Older Omega materials should be read as historical, methodological, or exploratory unless explicitly reauthorized.

## Current Allowed Framing

Current public framing may describe:

- working definitions;
- claim boundaries;
- empirical-readiness requirements;
- historical status of earlier Omega materials;
- future conditions that would be required before empirical claims.

This framing should not be converted into an empirical result.

## Requirements For Any Future Empirical Claim

Any future empirical claim would require, before evaluation:

- fixed definitions;
- independent event labels;
- non-circularity checks;
- sufficient sample size;
- a validation design;
- clear treatment of null or weak outcomes.

No future empirical claim should be made from historical Omega materials alone.
