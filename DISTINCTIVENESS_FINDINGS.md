# VOT Distinctiveness — Findings on Existing Data

Status: findings record (existing-data results against the comparison plan)
Date: 2026-06-30
Claim level: bounded existing-data results; upgrades no claim

This document reports what happened when the distinctiveness tests in [Distinctiveness Head-to-Head](DISTINCTIVENESS_HEAD_TO_HEAD.md) were attempted on existing public data. It is the honest current answer to the central risk stated there — that VOT may be coherent but a **redescription** of nearby theories, especially Sense of Coherence (SOC). For the global status see [Current Status](CURRENT_STATUS.md); for routes see [Empirical Frontier](EMPIRICAL_FRONTIER.md).

## Headline

On existing data the framework's **`O/M/A` construct** behaves as a **structured redescription** of Sense-of-Coherence / well-being covariance. Construct distinctiveness is **not yet established**, and — importantly — it is **not answerable from existing data at all**, because the framework's `O/M/A` and the nearest rival (SOC) are built from the same items. The decisive test requires a purpose-built study with **disjoint** instruments.

This concerns only the **construct-distinctiveness of O/M/A versus neighbors**. It does not bear on the conceptual reframing (pleasure ≠ value; suffering ≠ reversed pleasure) or on the archived L4 structural-proxy record, which are unaffected.

## What was tested, and what it returned

### 1. Internal incremental validity (Test D1, existing survey data)

| Test | Benchmark | Result | Reading |
|---|---|---|---|
| `E_bar` increment (distress) | standard well-being / neighbor blocks | ≈ 0 added out-of-sample | no distinct value over standard composites |
| deficit / anchor increment (distress) | neighbor blocks | small positive (≈ +0.014 CV R²) | weak internal pass |
| deficit / anchor increment (distress) | a flexible nonlinear function of the **same inputs** | negative (~ −0.004) | fails the strict same-input benchmark |
| cross-dataset reproduction | held-out / second dataset | in-sample positive, **out-of-sample not reproduced** | no stable increment |

Methodological caveat: because `E_bar` and the deficit/anchor terms are all functions of the same input blocks, these are an **internal decomposition**, not a clean external test. A flexible nonlinear function of the inputs absorbs any function of those inputs. The clean test therefore requires rivals measured on **disjoint** items.

### 2. The structural blocker (why existing data cannot settle it)

In every reachable existing dataset, the items used to build `O/M/A` and the items of the nearest rival overlap or coincide. The clearest case: in an item-level SOC-13 dataset, the framework's `O/M/A` **are** the SOC-13 subscales and the SOC total is the mean of the same items. There is nothing disjoint left to separate them. This is exactly the failure condition named in the head-to-head plan: *"O/M/A items are semantically indistinguishable from SOC items."*

### 3. The one external sub-test the data supports (functional form)

Where a validated SOC scale exists (n = 226), we asked whether the framework's specific functional form (bottleneck, imbalance, low-component penalty, geometric mean, interaction product) adds out-of-sample value over the plain SOC sum:

| Outcome | plain SOC sum | VOT functional form added | Δ out-of-sample R² [5th, 95th pct] |
|---|---|---|---|
| well-being (depression-reversed) | 0.283 | — | **−0.037** [−0.064, −0.003] |
| self-rated health | 0.140 | — | **−0.072** [−0.120, −0.027] |

The VOT-specific form does not add and slightly **reduces** out-of-sample prediction relative to the plain sum (intervals exclude zero). Bounded reading: at this sample size and dataset, the added nonlinear structure over-fits rather than helps. It does not refute the form, but the form is **not justified** on current data and should be cleared or dropped.

## Honest current reading

- **Distinctiveness:** not established, and not testable on existing data (shared items). → purpose-built D3 with disjoint instruments is the only path.
- **Functional form:** the bottleneck/imbalance machinery loses to the simple sum where a validated rival exists; justify it or drop it.
- **Net label:** structured redescription of SOC / well-being covariance, pending D3.

This matches, rather than escapes, the head-to-head **downgrade rules**: where SDT/SOC/appraisal absorb O/M/A and no holdout increment remains, the correct status is *redescription / measurement vocabulary*, not a distinct theory — until a disjoint-instrument study shows otherwise.

## Next decisive step

A minimal, single-wave, purpose-built study that measures `O/M/A`/Anchor and the rival constructs (SOC, SDT need-satisfaction, mastery, meaning, affect) with **non-overlapping** items, pre-registering: (1) O/M/A factor separation, (2) incremental validity over rivals, (3) pleasure/suffering asymmetry, (4) whether the nonlinear form beats the additive sum, and (5) a holdout confirmation — with a pre-committed redescription-downgrade path if the increments do not appear.

## Boundary

This document reports bounded existing-data results. It does not establish or refute construct distinctiveness (existing data cannot test it), measures no Omega or O/M/A, and upgrades no claim. The functional-form result is bounded to one dataset (n = 226).
