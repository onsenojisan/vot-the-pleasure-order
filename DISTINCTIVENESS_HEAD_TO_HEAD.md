# VOT Distinctiveness Head-to-Head

Status: public-facing anti-redundancy audit  
Date: 2026-06-19  
Claim level: comparison plan, not validation evidence  

> This is the comparison **plan**. For what happened when these tests were attempted on existing data — including the Sense-of-Coherence absorption result — see [Distinctiveness Findings](DISTINCTIVENESS_FINDINGS.md) (2026-06-30).

## Purpose

This document addresses the central distinctiveness risk for Valence Order Theory / The Pleasure Order:

> VOT may be coherent but redundant.

The largest risk is not that the theory is obviously false. The largest risk is that its surviving claims can be fully restated by existing frameworks such as free-energy / predictive processing, viability theory, capability approaches, self-determination theory, sense of coherence, appraisal theory, resilience, antifragility, or process theories.

Therefore this document states:

1. what VOT shares with nearby theories;
2. what VOT would have to add to be distinctive;
3. what tests are required;
4. what results would force VOT to be downgraded to a synthesis, redescription, or parameterization.

## Current VOT Claim Boundary

The current safe form is narrow:

- `C`: local coherence.
- `dC/dt`: local coherence flux, usable only as a qualified local pleasure signal.
- `Omega`: directionality potential, the capacity to revise, reverse, repair, and reorganize future trajectories.
- `dOmega/dt`: long-term directionality flux.
- `dC/dt` and `dOmega/dt` are not identical.
- pleasure is not value itself.
- value is tied to preservation or recovery of directionality, not hedonic intensity.
- suffering is not merely reversed pleasure; the focused candidate is unanchored loss or deficit.
- a bridge from local pleasure/coherence to later directionality is conditional, not assumed.

Current empirical status:

- D2/D2+ proxy support only.
- MIDUS distress-side deficit signal is small and domain-limited.
- GSS does not strongly reproduce the signal.
- direct O/M/A D3 has not yet been run.
- the `dC/dt -> dOmega/dt` bridge is specified but untested.

## What Would Count As Distinctive

VOT should not claim distinctiveness merely by renaming known constructs.

The strongest candidate for distinctiveness is the conjunction:

```text
Local coherence/pleasure flux is not value.
It predicts later directionality only when anchored by repairability,
corrective update, continuity, and future option preservation.

Pleasure and suffering are asymmetric:
  ordered pleasure = anchored gain
  suffering = unanchored loss

Harmful or rigid high-coherence states are explicitly blocked from
positive value or directionality claims.
```

VOT is distinctive only if this conjunction performs empirical or formal work that nearby theories do not already perform as well.

## Head-to-Head Summary

| Nearby framework | Overlap with VOT | VOT distinctive candidate | If this happens, VOT loses |
|---|---|---|---|
| Free-energy / predictive processing | Surprisal, uncertainty, prediction-error reduction, coherence | VOT separates local coherence flux from long-term directionality and blocks harmful high-coherence states | prediction-error/free-energy terms explain pleasure, suffering, and directionality as well as VOT terms |
| Viability theory | viable set, recovery capacity, future trajectory preservation | VOT adds experience-facing O/M/A, Anchor, and pleasure/suffering asymmetry | viability or recovery metrics explain outcomes; O/M/A and Anchor add nothing |
| Capability approach | value as freedom, capability, and future option space | VOT adds local valence dynamics, unanchored suffering, and bridge tests | capability/freedom measures absorb Anchor, Agency, Omega proxies, and outcomes |
| Self-determination theory | autonomy, competence, relatedness, need satisfaction/frustration | VOT adds O/M/A, Anchor, deficit asymmetry, and harmful-order cases | SDT satisfaction/frustration fully absorbs O/M/A, Anchor, and deficit terms |
| Sense of coherence | comprehensibility, manageability, meaningfulness | VOT adds valence asymmetry and local-to-long-term bridge | SOC explains factor structure and outcomes; VOT adds no holdout increment |
| Appraisal theory | control, coping, certainty, goal relevance, agency, meaning | VOT adds formal Anchor/Deficit parameterization and harmful-order gate | appraisal dimensions predict outcomes equally or better |
| Resilience / antifragility | perturbation response, recovery, adaptation, robustness | VOT links subjective local valence to repairability and future options | resilience metrics explain directionality without VOT-specific terms |
| Process / non-attachment frameworks | non-clinging, self-maintenance, process order | VOT attempts operational measurement and failure-capable tests | VOT remains metaphorical and yields no unique tests |

## Free-Energy / Predictive Processing

### Shared Territory

This is the most serious overlap.

Earlier VOT formulations that define valence as something like entropy, uncertainty, surprisal, or prediction-error reduction are close to free-energy or predictive-processing accounts of valence.

The overlap includes:

- positive valence as reduction in uncertainty, surprisal, free energy, or prediction error;
- negative valence as unresolved error or failed prediction;
- coherence as predictive integration;
- learning or model update as repair.

This overlap must be acknowledged, not hidden.

### VOT's Distinctive Candidate

VOT can remain distinct only if it refuses the simple equation:

```text
pleasure = prediction-error reduction = value
```

Instead, VOT's distinctive claim is:

```text
local dC/dt or error reduction is only locally pleasurable.
It is directionality-positive only when it preserves repairability,
corrective update, continuity, and future options.
```

Expected divergence cases:

- addictive reward loops;
- rigid ideological certainty;
- coercive social order;
- brittle optimization;
- pleasure that narrows future agency;
- prediction-error reduction through self-sealing closure.

These may reduce local error or raise local coherence while damaging directionality.

### Required Test

Compare:

```text
FEP model:
  outcome ~ prediction_error_change + uncertainty + affect + covariates

VOT bridge model:
  outcome ~ dC_dt_lag + persistence + corrective_update
            + continuity + future_option_preservation
            + harmful_order_gate + covariates

Combined model:
  outcome ~ FEP terms + VOT terms + covariates
```

Primary question:

> Do VOT bridge/anchor terms add holdout predictive value beyond free-energy or prediction-error terms?

VOT loses if:

- FEP terms fully absorb VOT terms.
- VOT only restates prediction-error reduction.
- harmful high-coherence cases are not separated.
- `dC/dt` alone performs as well as the conditional bridge.

Safe wording:

> VOT is compatible with predictive-processing accounts but treats local error/coherence dynamics as insufficient for value unless directionality-preserving conditions are met.

Forbidden wording:

> VOT proves free-energy theory wrong.

> VOT is independent of free-energy theory.

> `V = -dH/dt` is already a validated VOT-specific result.

## Viability Theory

### Shared Territory

Viability theory concerns whether a system can remain within a viable set under constraints. This overlaps strongly with:

- future option preservation;
- recoverability;
- avoiding irreversible collapse;
- maintaining possible trajectories;
- retaining adaptive capacity under perturbation.

VOT's `Omega` is close to a psychological or structural version of viability capacity.

### VOT's Distinctive Candidate

VOT adds an experience-facing layer:

- direct O/M/A;
- Anchor;
- pleasure/suffering asymmetry;
- local valence flux versus longer-term directionality.

VOT is not distinct if it merely renames viability as `Omega`.

### Required Test

Compare:

```text
Viability model:
  directionality_endpoint ~ viability_margin + recovery_capacity + constraint_slack

VOT model:
  directionality_endpoint ~ dC_dt_lag + Anchor + O/M/A
                            + corrective_update + future_option_preservation

Combined model:
  directionality_endpoint ~ viability terms + VOT terms
```

VOT loses if:

- viability margin or recovery metrics fully explain later directionality.
- O/M/A and Anchor add no incremental prediction.
- `Omega` cannot be defined independently from viability outcomes.

## Capability Approach

### Shared Territory

Capability theory treats value as real freedom or capability, not pleasure or utility. This overlaps with:

- value not equal to pleasure;
- future possibilities;
- agency;
- option preservation;
- freedom to act and revise.

VOT's `Omega` can look like a dynamic capability or option-value construct.

### VOT's Distinctive Candidate

VOT adds:

- local pleasure/coherence dynamics;
- suffering as unanchored loss;
- explicit misfire cases where pleasure narrows capability;
- a possible bridge from subjective dynamics to directionality.

### Required Test

Compare:

```text
Capability model:
  outcome ~ capability + freedom + option measures + covariates

VOT model:
  outcome ~ O/M/A + Anchor + Deficit_Load + Unanchored_Deficit

Combined model:
  outcome ~ capability terms + VOT terms
```

VOT loses if:

- capability or freedom measures absorb Anchor and Agency.
- VOT does not improve distress asymmetry or ordered pleasure prediction.
- `Omega` is indistinguishable from capability language.

## Self-Determination Theory

### Shared Territory

SDT is a primary competitor because:

- autonomy overlaps Agency;
- competence overlaps Agency and Order;
- relatedness overlaps Meaning and Anchor;
- need frustration predicts distress;
- need satisfaction predicts wellbeing.

Any D3 test that does not include SDT is too weak.

### VOT's Distinctive Candidate

VOT must add:

- O/M/A factor structure beyond SDT dimensions;
- Anchor as repair/future-option condition;
- Unanchored_Deficit as a suffering-side asymmetric term;
- maladaptive pleasure distinction.

### Required Test

Compare:

```text
B1: outcome ~ covariates + SDT + SOC + PANAS + control + meaning
B2: outcome ~ B1 + O + M + A + Anchor
B3: outcome ~ B1 + E_bar + Deficit_Load + Unanchored_Deficit + Anchored_Gain
```

VOT loses if:

- SDT satisfaction/frustration fully absorbs O/M/A and Anchor.
- need frustration explains unanchored suffering as well as Unanchored_Deficit.
- no holdout increment remains.

Safe wording:

> SDT is not background noise; it is a primary absorption test.

## Sense Of Coherence

### Shared Territory

SOC has three components:

- comprehensibility;
- manageability;
- meaningfulness.

This is close to:

- Order;
- Agency;
- Meaning.

SOC is therefore a direct threat to O/M/A distinctiveness.

### VOT's Distinctive Candidate

VOT must show:

- O/M/A are not merely SOC relabeled.
- Anchor/Deficit terms add distress-specific predictive value beyond SOC.
- pleasure/suffering asymmetry is not captured by SOC total.

### Required Test

Compare:

```text
SOC model:
  P_ordered, S_unanchored ~ SOC_total + PANAS + covariates

VOT model:
  P_ordered, S_unanchored ~ O/M/A + Anchor + Deficit terms + PANAS + covariates

Combined model:
  P_ordered, S_unanchored ~ SOC_total + VOT terms + PANAS + covariates
```

VOT loses if:

- SOC explains the outcomes and VOT adds no holdout increment.
- O/M/A factor separation collapses when SOC is included.
- O/M/A items are semantically indistinguishable from SOC items.

## Appraisal Theory

### Shared Territory

Appraisal theories already link emotion to:

- goal relevance;
- control;
- coping potential;
- certainty;
- agency;
- meaning;
- responsibility.

This overlaps heavily with O/M/A and Anchor.

### VOT's Distinctive Candidate

VOT must add a specific structure:

- pleasure side: anchored gain;
- suffering side: unanchored loss;
- deficit/bottleneck asymmetry;
- local pleasure can be maladaptive when it closes future options.

### Required Test

Compare:

```text
Appraisal model:
  outcome ~ certainty + control + coping + goal relevance + agency + affect

VOT model:
  outcome ~ O/M/A + Anchor + Deficit_Load + Unanchored_Deficit

Combined model:
  outcome ~ appraisal terms + VOT terms
```

VOT loses if:

- appraisal terms absorb Anchor and Unanchored_Deficit.
- VOT's terms cannot be separated from control, coping, certainty, or goal relevance.

Safe wording:

> VOT may be a constrained parameterization of appraisal structure unless it shows unique asymmetry and bridge behavior.

## Resilience, Antifragility, And Ecological Regulation

### Shared Territory

These frameworks already describe:

- recovery after perturbation;
- adaptation;
- robustness;
- improved function after stress;
- path dependence;
- persistence under disturbance.

VOT's bridge supports are close:

- persistence;
- corrective update;
- continuity;
- future option preservation.

### VOT's Distinctive Candidate

VOT adds:

- subjective local valence/coherence flux;
- suffering as unanchored loss;
- harmful-order gate;
- link between felt pleasure and long-term directionality.

### Required Test

Compare:

```text
Resilience model:
  later_function ~ baseline_function + perturbation + recovery_slope + variability

VOT bridge model:
  later_directionality ~ dC_dt_lag + bridge supports + harmful_order_gate
```

VOT loses if:

- resilience metrics fully explain later directionality.
- local valence/coherence adds no information.
- harmful-order gate is unnecessary.

## Minimal Distinctiveness Tests

### Test D1: Construct Absorption

Question:

> Do O/M/A and Anchor survive beyond SDT, SOC, PANAS, perceived control, and meaning?

Pass:

- purpose-built O/M/A items separate psychometrically;
- O/M/A + Anchor add holdout prediction beyond competitors.

Fail:

- competitors absorb all predictive value;
- O/M/A collapses into one general wellbeing/control/meaning factor.

### Test D2: Asymmetry Against General Affect

Question:

> Does Unanchored_Deficit predict suffering better than a symmetric wellbeing/affect model?

Pass:

- suffering-side improvement survives holdout;
- the same term does not merely predict all negative affect;
- ordered pleasure and unanchored suffering show asymmetric structure.

Fail:

- general negative affect, SDT frustration, SOC, appraisal, or pain-domain constructs explain the result as well.

### Test D3: Bridge Against Free-Energy And Resilience

Question:

> Does Candidate 2 beat `dC/dt` alone, prediction-error change, generic recovery, and resilience metrics?

Pass:

- bridge terms add holdout prediction;
- harmful high-coherence cases are blocked;
- endpoint independence is preserved.

Fail:

- simple error reduction, trend, amplitude, or recovery explains the endpoint.

## Minimum Increment Rule

For public claim discipline, a VOT-specific increment should not be treated as meaningful merely because it is statistically nonzero.

Minimum public pass language should require:

- preregistered or holdout evaluation;
- strong competitor block included;
- at least small but stable incremental performance beyond competitors;
- directionally correct behavior in harmful-order or misfire cases;
- explicit reporting of null or failed tests.

If the increment is unstable, domain-limited, or not reproduced, the correct status is:

> preliminary motivation, not distinctiveness.

## Downgrade Rules

VOT should be downgraded to a synthesis, redescription, or parameterization if:

- free-energy / predictive-processing terms absorb bridge behavior;
- viability or capability terms absorb `Omega` and future-option claims;
- SDT/SOC/appraisal terms absorb O/M/A, Anchor, and deficit terms;
- general affect explains the pleasure/suffering asymmetry;
- harmful high-coherence cases cannot be separated;
- direct O/M/A fails repeatedly with purpose-built items;
- no holdout or preregistered increment remains.

Possible downgrade labels:

- measurement vocabulary;
- synthesis of existing theories;
- two-layer framework without validated bridge;
- constrained parameterization of free-energy, viability, capability, SDT, SOC, or appraisal theory;
- unsupported conceptual framework.

## Safe Public Wording

Safe:

> VOT overlaps with several mature theories. Its distinctiveness is not established. It becomes distinctive only if its anchor, asymmetry, harmful-order, and bridge terms add stable predictive or explanatory value beyond those theories.

Also safe:

> If nearby theories explain the same results as well as VOT, VOT should be treated as a synthesis, redescription, or parameterization rather than a distinct theory.

Forbidden:

> VOT has already surpassed free-energy theory.

> VOT is independent of SDT, SOC, appraisal, viability, or capability theory.

> O/M/A is already proven distinct from existing constructs.

> Existing proxy evidence establishes VOT's uniqueness.

## Current Status

The current correct status is:

> Distinctiveness is a live risk, not a settled achievement.

The next legitimate upward move is not more vocabulary. It is direct measurement and head-to-head comparison with the closest rival theories.
