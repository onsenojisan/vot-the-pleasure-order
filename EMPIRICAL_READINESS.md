# Empirical Readiness

This document defines minimum readiness conditions for any future empirical claim related to VOT / The Pleasure Order or Omega.

Empirical readiness is a precondition for future evaluation. It is not an empirical result, a validation plan, a reproduction route, or a prediction protocol.

## Minimum Readiness Requirements

Before any future empirical claim is evaluated, the project would need:

- fixed definitions before evaluation;
- independent event or outcome labels;
- non-circularity checks;
- sufficient sample size;
- a validation design;
- treatment of null or weak outcomes;
- separation of exploratory, historical, and confirmatory materials.

These requirements must be fixed before interpretation. They should not be adjusted after seeing outcomes.

## Status Of Older Omega Materials

Older Omega empirical and reproducibility materials cannot by themselves support a current empirical claim.

They may be read as historical, methodological, or exploratory unless explicitly reauthorized. They should not be treated as current validation of Omega, VOT, The Pleasure Order, pleasure, or directionality.

## What A Future Claim Would Need To Specify

Any future empirical claim would need to specify:

- what is being measured;
- what outcome is being evaluated;
- what baseline or comparison is used;
- what would count as failure or non-support;
- what data and code are sufficient for independent review.

Without these elements, a result should not be treated as more than exploratory or historical.

## Boundary

This document does not add an empirical claim.

It does not claim that Omega, VOT, The Pleasure Order, pleasure, or directionality has been empirically established.

It records readiness conditions only.
