# Empirical Readiness

This document defines readiness conditions for empirical claims related to VOT / The Pleasure Order or Omega.

For the global current status, see [Current Status](CURRENT_STATUS.md). This document is narrower: it states what would be required before any stronger empirical claim could responsibly be made.

Empirical readiness is not a single level. The current public archive contains one bounded measurement-design result, while stronger validation claims remain future work.

## Minimum Requirements For Stronger Claims

Before any stronger empirical claim is evaluated, the project would need:

- fixed definitions before evaluation;
- independent event, outcome, or case labels;
- non-circularity checks;
- sufficient sample size or a justified public-data design;
- a validation design;
- treatment of null or weak outcomes;
- separation of exploratory, historical, archived, and confirmatory materials.

These requirements must be fixed before interpretation. They should not be adjusted after seeing outcomes.

## Status Of Older Omega Materials

Older Omega empirical and reproducibility materials cannot by themselves support a current direct validation claim.

They may be read as historical, methodological, or exploratory unless explicitly reauthorized. They should not be treated as current validation of Omega, VOT, The Pleasure Order, pleasure, or directionality.

## What A Stronger Future Claim Would Need To Specify

Any stronger empirical claim would need to specify:

- what is being measured;
- what outcome is being evaluated;
- what baseline or comparison is used;
- what would count as failure or non-support;
- what data and code are sufficient for independent review;
- whether the claim is structural-proxy support, direct measurement, causal identification, or theory confirmation.

Without these elements, a result should not be treated as more than bounded structural-proxy support, exploratory support, or historical evidence.
