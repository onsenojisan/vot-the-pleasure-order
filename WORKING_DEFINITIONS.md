# Working Definitions

This document records minimal working definitions for the restart phase of VOT / The Pleasure Order.

These definitions are public restart definitions. They do not add empirical claims, validation results, prediction claims, or causal claims.

## VOT

VOT is the active conceptual reconstruction layer in the current restart.

It is used here to organize definitions, claim boundaries, and empirical-readiness requirements. It should not be read as a validated theory.

## The Pleasure Order

The Pleasure Order is the public-facing name for the restart frame.

In this restart frame, pleasure should not be reduced to subjective feeling, reward, utility, or prediction-error reduction alone. Those may be relevant comparison terms, but they do not define the whole project.

This document does not claim that pleasure has been empirically explained.

## Pleasure

Pleasure is a concept under reconstruction.

Current public wording should separate:

- subjective feeling;
- structural coherence;
- directionality;
- operational or empirical proxies.

No biological, psychological, social, or civilizational claim should be made from this definition alone.

## Directionality

Directionality is a working concept related to the preservation, loss, correction, or updating of structure over time.

It is not currently presented as a measured universal law.

## Omega

Omega is historical, methodological, or future-conditional in the current public restart phase.

Omega is not currently validated, and it is not the current project center.

Earlier Omega materials may remain useful as historical records, methodological examples, or sources for future review, but they should not be read as current validation.

## Empirical Readiness

Empirical readiness means that a future empirical claim could be evaluated responsibly only after the required conditions are fixed before evaluation.

At minimum, those conditions include:

- fixed definitions;
- independent event labels;
- non-circularity checks;
- sufficient sample size;
- a validation design.

Empirical readiness is not itself an empirical result.

## Historical Omega Materials

Historical Omega materials are earlier empirical, reproducibility, execution, or exploratory materials from the Omega-facing phase of the project.

They should be read as historical, methodological, or exploratory unless explicitly reauthorized.

They should not be used as current validation of Omega, VOT, The Pleasure Order, pleasure, or directionality.

## O / M / A (retired terminology)

O (comprehensibility), M (meaning), and A (agency / manageability) were earlier working labels in this project.

They correspond to the three components of Antonovsky's Sense of Coherence (SOC): comprehensibility, meaningfulness, and manageability. Measured moment to moment, they are appraisal readouts in the sense of Scherer's Component Process Model (comprehensibility / control / meaning appraisals).

Because they are not separable from SOC — in existing data they are built from the same items, and the project's own functional form did not add over the plain SOC sum — O / M / A are retired as proprietary terms.

- Trait / dispositional context: use SOC (Antonovsky).
- Momentary / dynamic context: use appraisal terms (comprehensibility / control / meaning appraisals).

Earlier documents keep O / M / A as historical record. This is a terminology decision, not a new empirical claim.

VOT-specific terms not affected by this retirement: **Anchor** (a fall-back / viability component not present in SOC), the **cross-channel order operator**, and **Omega-hat**. These remain under evaluation and are not validated here.
